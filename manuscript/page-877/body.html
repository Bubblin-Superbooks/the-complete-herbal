Chapter XIII.

Of Troches.

  • The Latins call them Placentula, or little cakes, and the Greeks Prochikois, Kukliscoi, and Artiscoi; they are usually little round flat cakes, or you may make them square if you will.
  • Their first invention was, that powders being so kept might resist the intermission of air, and so endure pure the longer.
