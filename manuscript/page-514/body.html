Place. It grows on hills and heaths, yet where there is much grass, for therein it delights to grow.

Time. It is to be found only in April and May; for in June, when any hot weather comes, for the most part it is withered and gone.

Government and virtues. The Moon owns the herb. Moonwort is cold and drying more than Adder’s Tongue, and is therefore held to be more available for all wounds both inward and outward. The leaves boiled in red wine, and drank, stay the immoderate flux of women’s courses, and the whites. It also stays bleeding, vomiting, and other fluxes. It helps all blows and bruises, and to consolidate all fractures and dislocations.
