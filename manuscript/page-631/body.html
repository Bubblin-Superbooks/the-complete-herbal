The flowers grow at the tops of the stalks, with some shorter leaves with them, hooded after the same manner that the others are, but of a fair yellow colour, or in some paler, and in some more white. The seed is contained in large husks, and being ripe, will rattle or make a noise with lying loose in them. The root is small and slender, perishing every year.

Place. They grow in meadows and woods generally through this land.

Time. They are in flower from Midsummer until August be past, sometimes.

Government and virtues. They are both of them under the dominion of the Moon.
