Place. It grows on ditch banks, and sometimes in ditches, if they be dry, and in sandy grounds.

Time. It flowers about June or July, and abides green all the Winter.

Government and virtues. The Moon owns this herb also; and though authors cry out upon Alchymists, for attempting to fix quicksilver by this herb and Moonwort, a Roman would not have judged a thing by the success; if it be to be fixed at all, it is by lunar influence. The juice thereof taken in wine, or the decoction thereof drank, doth help the jaundice, although of long continuance, to drink thereof morning and evening, and abstain from other drink two or three hours after.
