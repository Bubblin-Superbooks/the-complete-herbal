Chapter IX.

Of Lohocks.

  • That which the Arabians call Lohocks, and the Greeks Eclegma, the Latins call Linctus, and in plain English signifies nothing else but a thing to be licked up.
  • They are in body thicker than a syrup, and not so thick as an electuary.
  • The manner of taking them is, often to take a little with a liquorice stick, and let it go down at leisure.
