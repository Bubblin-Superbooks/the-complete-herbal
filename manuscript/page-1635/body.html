Chapter X.

Of Medicines provoking urine.

The causes by which urine is suppressed are many.

  • By too much drying, or sweating, it may be consumed.
  • By heat or inflammation of the reins, or passages whereby it passes from the reins, it may be stopped by compression.

Urine is the thinnest part of blood, separated from the thickest part in the reins.
